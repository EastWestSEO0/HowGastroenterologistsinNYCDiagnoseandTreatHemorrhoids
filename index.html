What Exactly Does a Gastroenterologist Do?

A gastroenterologist is a physician who specializes in diagnosing, treating, and preventing conditions affecting the digestive system. These conditions include heartburn, acid reflux, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), colon polyps, liver disease, and—one of the most common disorders of all—hemorrhoids.

Becoming a gastroenterologist requires over a decade of medical education and hands-on clinical training. This includes medical school, residency in internal medicine, and then a three-year fellowship specifically focused on gastrointestinal health. This level of expertise allows them to diagnose conditions that are often difficult to identify without proper testing, such as endoscopy, colonoscopy, capsule endoscopy, and anorectal examinations.

Gastroenterologists are also uniquely equipped to provide hemorrhoid treatment, especially for patients whose symptoms do not improve with home care. Because hemorrhoids are so closely connected to the rectum and colon, these specialists are best positioned to offer accurate diagnosis and safe, effective medical treatment.

Can a Gastroenterologist Treat Rectal Bleeding from Hemorrhoids?

Yes. A gastroenterologist is a digestive health specialist trained in treating hemorrhoids and diagnosing the causes of rectal bleeding. While many cases of mild rectal bleeding result from hemorrhoids, a qualified specialist will also rule out other conditions like fissures, polyps, or more serious diseases during your hemorrhoid treatment NYC evaluation.

Are There Minimally Invasive Hemorrhoid Treatment Options in NYC?

Absolutely. In New York City, patients seeking hemorrhoid treatment NYC from a trusted gastroenterologist NYC have access to multiple minimally invasive procedures. Common treatments include rubber band ligation, infrared coagulation therapy, and sclerotherapy—all of which can be performed in an office setting with minimal discomfort and little to no downtime.

How Do I Know if Rectal Bleeding Is from Hemorrhoids or Something More Serious?

Rectal bleeding can occur for several reasons. While hemorrhoids are a very common cause, especially when associated with strain during bowel movements or irritation, only a gastroenterologist can determine the source definitively.

What Can I Do to Reduce the Risk of Hemorrhoids Repeating After Treatment?

After effective hemorrhoid treatment, your gastroenterologist may recommend long-term preventive steps to reduce recurrence. These often include increasing dietary fiber, staying well-hydrated, avoiding prolonged sitting on the toilet, practicing good bowel habits, and managing constipation or diarrhea.
